Semester 1 Impact Report

C-SHAW Peer Education Hub • June 2026

1. Executive Summary: The Problem & The Solution

The Catalyst: The 2025 APB Incident

In late 2025, an APB Executive was trying to tally up the final volunteer hours for the Peer Educators. Working through stacks of confusing, handwritten paper registers, a mistake was made with someone's hours. The debate that followed wasn't just about a math error, it showed us that the process was broken. Our Executives were getting burnt out doing basic arithmetic.

The Problem: The Old Way

The paper-based tracking system was slowing us down. Executives were spending hours manually counting time, trying to read messy handwriting, and hunting for lost registers.

Communication was another big bottleneck. We relied heavily on WhatsApp groups to share opportunities, but important event details were constantly getting buried under hundreds of chat messages. Dedicated volunteers were missing out on events just because they didn't see a text in time.

The Solution: The C-SHAW Hub

We realized we didn't just need a digital register; we needed a better way to manage everything. Here is what the new platform helps us achieve today:

  • Easier Admin: The system automatically tracks and counts every hour, minute and second volunteered as soon as a student is signed in.
  • Clear Communication: An automated email system notifies every active student as soon as a new event is posted, cutting out the WhatsApp noise.
  • Live Updates: Coordinators now have a real-time view of the whole program, so we don't have to wait for end-of-month to compile spreadsheets.

3. Outcomes & Behavioral Impact

The true success of the C-SHAW Hub isn't just in data storage, it is in how the technology has fundamentally transformed student behavior and program culture.

The Gamification Effect

Before the Hub, tracking hours was a background chore. By introducing a dynamic Leaderboard and personalized stats, we turned volunteering into a healthy, highly visible competition.

Students can now see exactly where they rank within their specific campus, alongside their global standing across the 4 campuses. This dual-view sparked friendly cross-campus rivalries and motivated volunteers to actively seek out extra hours to hit the prestigious 80-hour mark—a milestone four of our top educators have already smashed. 4. Key Challenges & The Honest Reality

Transitioning a program of this size from paper to a strict digital ecosystem wasn't without its hurdles. Being transparent about these friction points has been critical to improving the platform.

System & Infrastructure Hurdles

  • Campus Connectivity: Certain event venues on campus have severe Wi-Fi dead zones, occasionally causing delays during high-volume sign-ins.
  • Early Deployment Bugs: The first two weeks exposed edge-case bugs that required immediate late-night hotfixes to ensure data integrity wasn't compromised.

User Adoption & Change Management

  • Breaking the WhatsApp Habit: Getting students to transition to officially RSVPing through the Hub required enforcing a strict "No RSVP, No Entry" policy to drive adoption.
  • The Punctuality Pushback: Because the system automatically timestamps every check-in, executives could no longer turn a blind eye to latecomers. This shift caused initial friction but ultimately drove a noticeable improvement in punctuality.

5. Strategic Recommendations & The Road Ahead

Based on system analytics and user feedback, here is our roadmap for tightening operations and scaling our impact in Semester 2.

Long-Term Hardware Vision

Currently, the system relies on our Executives using their personal smartphones. While this works perfectly for our current rollout, it does rely heavily on personal devices and battery life.

Looking ahead, my recommendation is to eventually invest in a dedicated, low-cost tablet for each campus. These would act as official, standalone "Check-In Kiosks", making the process even faster and looking highly professional.

To be clear, this is not an urgent request for this year. It is a roadmap suggestion for the years to come, to ensure the system remains sustainable as the program continues to grow.

Native Mobile App Development

A dedicated mobile application is currently the most highly requested feature from our Peer Educators. An app would allow for instant push notifications, easier profile access, and a much more seamless experience.

Designing, building, and maintaining a standalone app across both iOS and Android is a massive undertaking. To make this a reality in the future, I will need as much support and backing as possible to execute it properly.

Like the hardware vision, this is a long-term roadmap goal. I am eager to spearhead it, but it represents the next major evolution of the Hub once our web platform is fully matured.

Technical Roadmap

  • Security & Infrastructure: Tightening backend security protocols during the June maintenance window to ensure the platform remains fast as our user base grows.
  • Feature Implementation: Actively building and deploying the top-requested quality-of-life features sourced directly from the students' feedback tab.

A Proof of Concept for Clinic-Wide Innovation

The success of the C-SHAW Hub proves a critical point: when we automate tedious manual tasks, we free up our people to actually lead, support, and execute their core missions.

This platform demonstrates how we can inspire further digital transformation across the clinic. By identifying other areas bogged down by manual paperwork and replacing them with smart systems, we can establish this organization as an absolute leader in technological innovation and operational efficiency.

6. Wishlist: Empowering Digital Skills

Please Note: The initiatives outlined in this section are currently unconfirmed. They represent a personal wishlist of proposed ideas designed to further encourage, motivate, and upskill our Peer Educators.

The Hub has solved our immediate administrative problems, but my ultimate vision goes beyond just managing volunteers. I want to use this momentum to actively teach digital skills to our team.

Introduction to Coding Classes

Instead of our Peer Educators just being users of the platform, I would love to host optional workshops where they can learn the basics of how the Hub was built. Teaching them foundational web development could spark an interest in tech and open new doors for them beyond their current studies.

The Open Source & GitHub Initiative

I hope to eventually set up a shared GitHub repository for the team. It would serve as a collaborative space where PEs can learn version control, share ideas, and start contributing to small automation scripts that make life on their specific campuses easier.

The Developer Bounty Program (Self-Funded)

To truly encourage hands-on learning, I would love to introduce a self-funded "Bounty Program" in the future.

If a Peer Educator spots a critical bug, designs a viable new feature, or learns enough to actually write a piece of code that improves the Hub, I want to personally reward them with a small financial bounty out of my own pocket. It is simply a practical, fun way to incentivize digital literacy and give back to the peers who make this entire program possible.
